Preseason Cycle – Week 7

This week will introduce a new strength training tool called fatigue percent sets. This is a tool that allows for autoregulation. When you have a good day you end up doing more volume and when you have a bad day you’ll do less volume.

Here is an example:

Back Squat 5RM @ RPE 9 then 4-6% Fatigue Sets

  • Work up to a 5RM at RPE 9
    • 345 @ RPE 8
    • 365 @ RPE 8.5
    • 375 @ RPE 9
  • Then perform sets with 4-6% less until that weight becomes an RPE 9
    • Select weight between 352-360 (if you feel good, choose the higher weight)
    • 360×5 @ RPE 8, keep going…
    • 360×5 @ RPE 8.5, keep going…
    • 360×5 @ RPE 8.5, keep going…
    • 360×5 @ RPE 9, this is where you would terminate and move on
    • You might perform 1 set or 10-15 sets, depending on the day and your fatigue.
    • Rest times should not be greater than 3-4 min. If you need to rest more than that, it is time to move on.

  • Breathing in Split: Hold bar overhead and perform deep breathing.
  • Tall Jerk: Watch video on Catalyst.
  • BTN Split Jerk + Narrow Grip OHS: Use a narrow grip that is comfortable yet challenging.
  • Strict OHP: Work to RM at given RPE then perform fatigue sets.
